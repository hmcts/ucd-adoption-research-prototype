Apply to adopt a child

There are 4 types of adoption.

  1. Adopting a child placed in your care – adopting a child who's living with you following a court placement order.
  2. Adopting your step-child – adopting your partner's child, usually with the consent of the other parent.
  3. Adopting from overseas – adopting a child from certain countries.
  4. Adopting a relinquished baby – adopting a child who's been voluntarily placed for adoption by its parents.

General adoption eligibility

To be adopted, a child must:

  • be under 18 on the date you submit your application
  • not be, or have ever been, married or in a civil partnership

You can only apply if you, and your partner, if relevant:

  • have lived in the UK for at least 12 months
  • are 21 or older

You or your partner must also be habitually resident in the United Kingdom, Isle of Man or Channel Islands.

There is a fee of £183 per application.

You can apply to adopt more than one child. You will only be charged one application fee. You might be able to [get help paying court fees](Get help paying court and tribunal fees - GOV.UK (www.gov.uk)).
